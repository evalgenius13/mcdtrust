The Solution

Accessible dynasty trust structures through nonprofit-brokerage partnerships with flexible commitment periods designed to build disciplined investment behavior.

Structured Trust Products

This model offers standardized trust instruments with features designed for scalability and accessibility.

Accessible Entry
Minimum investment thresholds starting at modest amounts to serve a broad market.
Streamlined Process
Simplified, automated account opening to reduce legal complexity and friction.
Flexible Terms
A tiered structure with lock periods ranging from 7 to 100 years, designed to build investment discipline while accommodating diverse, long-term financial horizons.
Institutional Efficiency
Dramatically reduced administrative costs through a standardized partnership model.

Implementation Framework

Strategic partnerships between nonprofit organizations and registered broker-dealers enable scalable dynasty trust access through proven financial infrastructure.

🏦

Regulated Custody

Licensed broker-dealers provide custody services, investment management (primarily in low-cost index funds), and regulatory compliance within existing financial frameworks.

⚖️

Fiduciary Governance

Tax-exempt organizations serve as trustee entities, ensuring fiduciary responsibility, long-term beneficiary advocacy, and mission alignment.

🔗

Independent Verification

A distributed public ledger creates a permanent, tamper-proof record of all trust activities and audits, ensuring transparency that is independent of any single institution or government.

Sustainable Funding Model

The nonprofit trustee is sustained through a transparent, industry-standard revenue-sharing agreement with its brokerage partners, ensuring long-term operational viability without compromising its fiduciary duty.

Operational Note: This B2B (Business-to-Business) model is critical. The brokerage treats the nonprofit as a single, large institutional client, avoiding the high cost of servicing millions of small individual accounts. The nonprofit manages all user-facing support and advocacy, creating institutional efficiency.

Expected Outcomes

Structured savings programs with predetermined lock periods cultivate long-term investment behavior while providing access to institutional-quality wealth management strategies.

Behavioral Impact: Lock period structures promote delayed gratification and systematic wealth accumulation, particularly benefiting communities with limited access to traditional wealth management services. Historical market performance suggests significant long-term wealth creation potential through disciplined investment approaches.
Market Considerations: Past performance does not guarantee future results. All investment products carry inherent market risks. This model represents one approach to addressing wealth concentration through expanded access to proven wealth-building instruments.
